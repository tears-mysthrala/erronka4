## 2. Purpose and Scope

### 2.1 Purpose

This Acceptable Use Policy (AUP) defines acceptable and unacceptable use of Zabala Gailetak's information technology resources. The purpose is to:
- Protect the organization from security risks
- Ensure compliance with legal and regulatory requirements
- Maintain productivity and professional work environment
- Protect company reputation and assets
- Clarify employee rights and responsibilities

### 2.2 Scope

This policy applies to:
- **All Users:** Employees (permanent, temporary, contract), consultants, contractors, visitors, and third parties
- **All Devices:** Company-owned and personal devices (BYOD) used for business purposes
- **All Resources:** IT systems, networks, email, internet, software, mobile devices, cloud services, and data
- **All Locations:** Office, production facility, home, remote work, customer sites, travel

### 2.3 Compliance

Compliance with this policy is mandatory. Violations may result in:
- Disciplinary action (verbal warning, written warning, suspension, termination)
- Revocation of access privileges
- Legal action (criminal or civil prosecution)
- Financial liability for damages

Employees must acknowledge understanding of this policy annually.

## 3. General Principles

### 3.1 Business Purpose

IT resources are provided for business purposes. Limited personal use is permitted provided it:
- Does not interfere with work duties
- Does not consume significant resources
- Does not violate any policy provision
- Complies with all laws and regulations

Zabala Gailetak reserves the right to monitor, access, and disclose content on company systems to ensure policy compliance, investigate misconduct, or comply with legal obligations.

### 3.2 Professional Conduct

Users must:
- Act professionally and ethically
- Respect intellectual property rights
- Protect confidential information
- Avoid conflicts of interest
- Report security incidents and policy violations

### 3.3 No Expectation of Privacy

Users should have no expectation of privacy when using company IT resources. Zabala Gailetak reserves the right to:
- Monitor network traffic and system usage
- Review email, files, and internet activity
- Log access and authentication events
- Inspect devices (company-owned or BYOD with company data)

Monitoring is conducted in compliance with Spanish labor law and employee privacy rights. Personal communications clearly marked as such will be respected where legally required.

## 4. Account and Authentication Management

### 4.1 User Accounts

**Account Issuance:**
- User accounts are issued by IT upon authorization from the user's manager
- Each user receives a unique username (no shared accounts)
- Accounts are personal and non-transferable

**Account Responsibilities:**
- Users are responsible for all activities under their account
- Users must not share their credentials with anyone
- Users must log off when leaving workstation unattended
- Users must report lost, stolen, or compromised credentials immediately

**Account Lifecycle:**
- Accounts are created within 24 hours of hire/onboarding
- Accounts are disabled immediately upon termination or extended leave
- Inactive accounts (>90 days) are automatically disabled
- Disabled accounts are deleted after 30 days (data archived per retention policy)

### 4.2 Password Requirements

See dedicated Password Policy (PWD-001) for detailed requirements. Summary:
- **Minimum length:** 12 characters (14 for privileged accounts)
- **Complexity:** Upper, lower, number, special character
- **Expiration:** 90 days (180 days if MFA enabled)
- **History:** Cannot reuse last 12 passwords
- **Prohibited:** Dictionary words, personal information, company name, common patterns

**Password Management:**
- Never write passwords down (use approved password manager)
- Never share passwords (including with IT staff or managers)
- Never use same password for multiple systems
- Change password immediately if compromise suspected

### 4.3 Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)

MFA is **required** for:
- Remote access (VPN)
- Administrative/privileged accounts
- Access to customer data or financial systems
- Cloud services (M365, AWS, GitHub)

MFA is **recommended** for:
- Standard user accounts (office workstations)

**Approved MFA Methods:**
1. Authenticator app (Microsoft Authenticator, Google Authenticator, Authy)
2. Hardware token (YubiKey for administrators)
3. SMS (least preferred, only if others unavailable)

**Prohibited:** Email-based MFA (same channel as primary authentication)

## 5. Computer and Workstation Use

### 5.1 Physical Security

**Workstation Security:**
- Lock screen when leaving desk (Windows+L or Ctrl+Alt+Delete ‚Üí Lock)
- Automatic screen lock after 5 minutes of inactivity
- Do not allow unauthorized persons to use your workstation
- Report lost or stolen equipment immediately

**Clean Desk Policy:**
- Secure or shred confidential documents when not in use
- Do not leave sensitive information visible on desk
- Lock offices and cabinets containing sensitive information
- Do not write down passwords

**Clean Screen Policy:**
- Position screens away from public view where possible
- Use privacy filters for mobile work in public spaces
- Close sensitive applications when not in use

### 5.2 Software Installation

**Approved Software:**
- Only IT-approved software may be installed on company devices
- Request software through IT helpdesk (software request form)
- IT maintains whitelist of approved applications

**Prohibited Software:**
- Unauthorized software, freeware, or shareware
- Pirated or unlicensed software
- Peer-to-peer file sharing (BitTorrent, etc.)
- Personal VPN or proxy services (circumventing corporate network)
- Cryptocurrency mining software
- Hacking tools (unless explicitly authorized for security team)
- Software from untrusted sources

**Browser Extensions:**
- Only approved browser extensions permitted
- Regularly review and remove unnecessary extensions
- Report suspicious extension behavior

### 5.3 System Configuration

**User Responsibilities:**
- Do not disable antivirus or endpoint protection
- Do not disable automatic updates
- Do not modify firewall settings
- Do not attempt to gain elevated privileges (privilege escalation)
- Do not tamper with security controls or logging

**Administrative Access:**
- Administrative rights granted only when business need justified
- Separate admin account for privileged tasks (not daily use)
- Admin access logged and periodically reviewed
- Temporary admin access expires after defined period

### 5.4 USB Drives and External Media

**Restrictions:**
- USB drives must be encrypted (company-provided encrypted drives)
- Scan all external media with antivirus before use
- Do not use personal USB drives for company data
- Do not connect unknown or untrusted USB devices

**Prohibited:**
- USB drives found in public places (potential attack vector)
- Unauthorized external hard drives
- Writeable optical media without approval

**Exceptions:** Approved for specific business needs with CISO authorization

## 6. Email and Communication

### 6.1 Email Use

**Acceptable Use:**
- Business communications with customers, suppliers, colleagues
- Professional external communications
- Limited personal use (brief, non-commercial)

**Prohibited Use:**
- Sending offensive, harassing, or discriminatory content
- Spam or mass unsolicited emails
- Chain letters or pyramid schemes
- Personal commercial activity (selling goods/services)
- Subscribing to non-business mailing lists using company email
- Impersonating others

**Email Security:**
- Be cautious of phishing emails (verify sender before clicking links)
- Do not open unexpected attachments, especially from unknown senders
- Verify requests for money transfers or credential changes (call requester)
- Report suspicious emails to security@zabalagailetak.com
- Use [EXTERNAL] indicator awareness (external emails auto-tagged)

**Confidential Information:**
- Encrypt emails containing Confidential or Highly Confidential data
- Verify recipient addresses before sending sensitive information
- Use BCC for mass external emails (protect recipient privacy)
- Do not email passwords or sensitive credentials

**Data Loss Prevention:**
- Automatic DLP scans outbound email
- Emails with credit card numbers, SSNs, or bulk PII blocked
- Policy violations generate alerts to security team

### 6.2 Email Retention

- Business emails retained according to Data Retention Schedule
- Users must not delete emails relevant to legal hold or investigations
- Personal emails should be minimal and clearly marked (subject: [PERSONAL])
- Automatic archival after 1 year to archive system

### 6.3 Instant Messaging and Collaboration Tools

**Approved Tools:**
- Slack (company workspace)
- Microsoft Teams (M365 tenant)
- Video conferencing: Zoom, Microsoft Teams

**Prohibited Tools:**
- Personal messaging apps for business communications (WhatsApp, Telegram, personal Slack)
- Unauthorized collaboration platforms
- Consumer-grade file sharing (personal Dropbox, WeTransfer)

**Usage Guidelines:**
- Professional tone in business communications
- No sharing of confidential information in public channels
- Assume all messages may be logged and reviewed
- Use private/direct messages for sensitive topics

**Company Social Media:**

- Employees may share official company posts

## 8. Mobile Devices and Remote Access

### 8.1 Mobile Devices (Smartphones, Tablets)

**Company-Owned Devices:**
- Subject to all company policies
- Must be enrolled in Mobile Device Management (MDM)
- Must use device passcode (minimum 6 digits or equivalent)
- Must enable encryption
- Must enable remote wipe capability
- Must install company-approved apps only
- Must report lost/stolen devices immediately (security team will remotely wipe)

**Bring Your Own Device (BYOD):**
- Personal devices may access company email with approval
- Must enroll in MDM (containerized approach - company data separated)
- Must meet minimum security requirements:
  - Device passcode enabled
  - Operating system up-to-date
  - Encryption enabled
- Company reserves right to remotely wipe company data (not personal data)
- BYOD devices subject to inspection if security incident suspected

**Mobile Device Security:**
- Do not jailbreak or root devices
- Install apps only from official app stores (Google Play, Apple App Store)
- Review app permissions before installing
- Do not connect to untrusted WiFi without VPN
- Be aware of shoulder surfing in public places

### 8.2 Remote Access and VPN

**VPN Required For:**
- Accessing internal company resources remotely
- Working from home or public locations
- Connecting to untrusted WiFi networks

**VPN Usage:**
- Use only company-provided VPN (OpenVPN, Cisco AnyConnect)
- Do not use personal VPN services (hides traffic from security monitoring)
- Do not share VPN credentials
- Disconnect VPN when not accessing company resources (performance)

**Remote Work Security:**
- Secure home WiFi (WPA3 or WPA2, strong password)
- Physical security of home office (lock away documents, devices)
- Family members should not use company devices
- Video call background awareness (no confidential info visible)

**Public WiFi:**
- Avoid public WiFi for sensitive work if possible
- Always use VPN on public WiFi
- Disable auto-connect to WiFi networks
- Verify WiFi network legitimacy (ask venue staff)

## 9. Data Classification and Handling

### 9.1 Data Classification

All information must be classified per Data Classification Policy:
- **Public:** No restrictions
- **Internal:** Internal use only
- **Confidential:** Restricted access, business impact if disclosed
- **Highly Confidential:** Severe business impact, strict controls

### 9.2 Handling Requirements

**Confidential and Highly Confidential Data:**
- Encrypt when stored or transmitted
- Access control - need-to-know basis
- Mark documents with classification label
- Do not send to personal email accounts
- Do not store on personal devices or unauthorized cloud services
- Shred paper documents before disposal
- Report loss or unauthorized disclosure immediately

**Customer Data (PII):**
- Subject to GDPR and data protection policies
- Access only when required for job duties
- Do not copy to personal devices or accounts
- Do not share with unauthorized third parties
- Report data breaches immediately (within 1 hour)

**Data Storage:**
- Store business data on approved company systems:
  - File server (on-premises)
  - OneDrive for Business (M365)
  - SharePoint (M365)
  - AWS S3 (for application data)
- Do not store company data on:
  - Personal cloud storage (personal Dropbox, Google Drive, iCloud)
  - Personal USB drives (unless encrypted company-provided)
  - Personal computers (except approved BYOD with MDM)

### 9.3 Data Transmission

**Email:**
- Encrypt emails containing Confidential/Highly Confidential data
- Use secure file transfer for large files (not email attachments >25 MB)

**File Sharing:**
- Approved: OneDrive/SharePoint sharing with expiration and passwords
- Prohibited: Consumer file sharing (WeTransfer, personal Dropbox links)

**Removable Media:**
- Use encrypted USB drives (company-provided)
- Hand-carry preferred over mail/courier for highly sensitive data

## 15. Monitoring and Enforcement

### 15.1 Monitoring

Zabala Gailetak monitors IT resource usage to:
- Ensure policy compliance
- Detect security incidents
- Investigate misconduct
- Comply with legal obligations
- Maintain system performance

**Monitoring Includes:**
- Network traffic analysis
- Firewall and IDS/IPS logs
- Authentication and access logs
- Email metadata and content (when justified)
- Web browsing history
- File access and modifications
- Endpoint security alerts

**Monitoring Principles:**
- Proportionate to business need
- Compliant with Spanish labor law (Workers' Statute)
- Employee privacy respected where possible
- Personal communications clearly marked as such are protected
- Monitoring conducted by authorized personnel only
- Results kept confidential (shared only on need-to-know basis)

### 15.2 Enforcement

**Violation Severity:**

**Minor Violations** (first offense or negligence):
- Examples: Weak password, unattended workstation, personal software installation
- Action: Verbal warning, retraining

**Serious Violations** (repeated or intentional):
- Examples: Sharing passwords, unauthorized software, policy non-compliance after warning, excessive personal use
- Action: Written warning, access restriction, performance improvement plan

**Severe Violations** (intentional or illegal):
- Examples: Data theft, hacking, malware distribution, illegal content, harassment, fraud
- Action: Immediate suspension or termination, legal prosecution, financial liability

**Disciplinary Process:**
1. Investigation (fact-finding, evidence collection)
2. Employee notification and opportunity to respond
3. Decision by HR and management
4. Documentation in employee file
5. Appeal process available (per labor law)

All disciplinary actions comply with Spanish labor law and collective bargaining agreements.

## Appendix B: Incident Reporting Quick Guide

**If you suspect a security incident:**

1. **STOP** - Do not continue activity that may be compromised
2. **DISCONNECT** - If malware suspected, disconnect from network (pull ethernet or disable WiFi)
3. **REPORT** - Contact security team immediately:
   - Email: security@zabalagailetak.com
   - Phone: +34 XXX XXX XXX (24/7)
   - In-person: CISO office
4. **PRESERVE** - Do not delete anything (evidence needed)
5. **DOCUMENT** - Note what happened, when, what you observed

**Common incidents to report:**
- Suspicious email or phishing attempt
- Malware alert or unusual system behavior
- Lost or stolen device
- Unauthorized access to account
- Accidental data disclosure
- USB drive or device found
- Policy violation observed

**When in doubt, report it.** Better safe than sorry. No one will be penalized for good-faith reporting.
